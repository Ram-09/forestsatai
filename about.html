The Operating System for
Wildfire Prevention

ForestSat was built from a simple thesis: megafires are not random natural disasters. They are the terminal phase of multi-year risk accumulation that can be observed, reconstructed — and prevented — using satellite Earth Observation, remote sensing, forest science and AI.

Our Mission

From Reacting to Fires to Preventing Them

The current wildfire management paradigm is built around reaction: detect fires, dispatch resources, fight the blaze, pay the bill. This paradigm is failing. Suppression costs in the US have grown from under $2B in 1994 to a requested $6.55B for FY2026. Insurance losses are growing at 12% per year globally. Eight of the ten costliest wildfire insured events on record have occurred in the past decade.

ForestSat was created to make prevention operational — not theoretical. To turn satellite data into decisions. To make the invisible visible: the slow-building fuel accumulation, the drying vegetation, the emerging danger that satellite systems can detect years before a spark becomes a megafire.

The strategic thesis is simple: identify where fire risk is accumulating → guide preventive action → verify it was done → respond intelligently when fires start → measure and recover. This is the ForestSat lifecycle.
